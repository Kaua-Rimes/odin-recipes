Smothered Cajun Chicken

This smothered Cajun chicken is an east slow cooker dinner. Chicken
breasts cook with onion, bell pepper, and Cajun seasoning, and green
beans are added in the last half hour. Serve over cooked rice.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 tablespoon plus 2 teaspoons Cajun
    seasoning, divided
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup water, or as needed
  • 1 tablespoon roasted chicken based, such
    as Better than Boullion
  • 2 cans green beans
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Season chicken with 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning; coat thoroughly and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Carefully add seasoned chicken
    to the skillet and sear just until all sides are browned, about 3 minutes. Remove ans set aside.
  3. Deglaze the skillet with a few tablespoons water and reduce liquid by half. Add garlic,
    onion, and bell pepper to the skillet. Cook and stir until softened,a bout 5 minutes.
  4. Transfer chicken and onion mixture to the slow cooker. Add in water, roasted chicken and and
    remaining 2 teaspoons Cajuns seasoning.
  5. Cover and cook on Low for 3 hours. Add green beans and butter; cook on High for 30 minutes
    more. Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve over rice.
